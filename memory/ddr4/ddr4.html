About:

DDR4 memory was introduced in 2014 around the release of Socket LGA 2011v3. Its improvements over DDR3 include: higher module density, lower voltage requirements, and higher data transfer rates. DDR4 is expected to be the standard by 2016. No direct successor to DDR4 is currently planned.
