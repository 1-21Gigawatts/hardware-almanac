DDR3

DDR3 memory was introduced in 2007, its primary benefit over DDR2 being the ability to transfer data twice as fast, thereby allowing more data to travel through it.
DDR3 DIMMs have a maximum capacity of 64 Gibibytes.
The first DDR4 modules were released in 2014, so DDR3 is in the process of being phased out.
